CityExplore

For the Mobile HCI course, we were divided into teams, and asked to design the prototype of an AR app to encourage outdoor activities with novel interactions.

Sketches and wireframes

As first thing, we researching existing applications, and came up with possible ideas for the app and the devices. I created personas and user stories to guide the design process, and we each came up with a couple of sketches for the app's UX. We performed heuristic evaluation on the sketches to figure out how to improve them, and we selected the best designs to carry forward.

Low level prototypes

We created low level prototypes of the designs, and asked a user sample to perform some tasks and fill a questionnaire. We also conducted a focus group to understand their opinions on the devices and interactions, and we selected the two best designs. One of them was my original design for an app that used AR glasses and an interactive ring. The app would encourage outdoor activity through gamification elements that would bring users to explore a city's cultural landmarks.

The final iteration

Finally, we created high level prototypes of the two designs, improving them with the suggestions from our user samples. We had a new user sample perform tasks on the prototypes and take part in semi-structured interviews. My prototype resulted to be the users' favourite, thanks to the ease of interaction through the ring and the non-invasive and intuitive UI.
