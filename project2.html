Butterfly Effect

For the third year software development project at University, my team was assigned to a client with an idea for a mobile app for suicide prevention. At the end of the course, we decided to keep working together as a team, and we are currently developing an app for overdose prevention.

The team project

Our client wanted a proof of concept, so during university we kept a very simple design due to time constraints. In the team, I was the product owner and front-end developer, but I ended up learning and working on back-end development, code reviews and coding sessions, testing and test driven development, CI/CD, issues and iterations, and demonstrations in customer's days.

Improving the design

When the project was over, we decided to keep working as a team, and to use what we learnt to make a similar app, but for overdose prevention. As first thing, I went back to sketching and prototyping. After talking with lecturers about creating a start-up however, we decided to suspend the project to focus on our final year at University.

Remaking the Butterly

Now that University is over, we are working on the app again. With an extra year of experience (and a new version of Flutter), we are set on creating a high-quality app that we hope could have a positive impact on society. Right now, I'm focused on creating an easy to use and intuitive interface, with a minimalist and modern UI.
